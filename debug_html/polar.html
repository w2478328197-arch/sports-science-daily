White Papers

The science behind Polar technology and smart coaching.

Our white papers offer you a thorough description of the scientific background of our technologies and the proprietary features in Polar products. They were written to give you a deeper understanding of how our products and solutions work. Each white paper comes with a short abstract and a full-length downloadable PDF.

Polar Elixirâ¢ OHR and SpO2

Polar Elixir white paper is about Polar optical heart rate (OHR) and blood oxygen saturation (SpO2) measurements. For both measurements the technical as well as physiological background for development are presented. White paper also includes methods and results on validation studies made against accepted reference methods. User benefits of SpO2 rewiev the meaning of results and gives indications on how to to use and interpret the measurement values.

Get the article

Polar Wrist-ECG

Polar Wrist-ECG white paper is documentation on physiological as well as technical background on electrogardiogram (ECG) measurement and tells about Polar development for wrist-ECG test. Measurement principle and technical solution are presented. White paper also includes methods and results on ECG signal quality and R-R-interval accuracy studies made against Holter monitoring. Discussion part of white paper review the performance of Polar wrist-ECG test and meaning of results.

Skin Temperature

Body temperature is a key indicator of health because changes in body temperature often signal disturbances in body homeostasis. This white paper provides an overview of body temperature regulation and highlights the employment of Polar technology for measuring skin temperature from wrist. It also describes how nightly skin temperature is visualized on Polar devices.

Work-Rest Guide

Polar Work-rest guide feature is based on heart rate measurement during training. The algorithm behind automatically detects the highest heart rate reached during the execution of an exercise set, and performs subsequent calculations to determine a suitable recovery heart rate. Using the Work-rest guide feature enhances both the overall effectiveness and the average cardiovascular work rate of a training session.

Walking Test

Polar Walking Test is a simple and safe way of estimating aerobic fitness, follow its development, and compare it to people of the same age and gender. The feature is built into Polar wrist units and is based on a widely used and recognized 2-kilometer walk test. By factoring in distance, heart rate, and personal demographics such as age, gender, height, and weight, Polar Walking Test predicts aerobic power (VO2max). To perform the test successfully, the user must walk continuously as âfast as possibleâ on a level surface for 15 minutes.
